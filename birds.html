Bird Species

Parrot

Parrots are intelligent, colorful birds with curved beaks and zygodactyl feet (two toes forward, two back). There are about 398 species worldwide, known for their ability to mimic sounds. They primarily eat seeds, nuts, fruit, and buds.

Ostrich

The ostrich is the world's largest bird, reaching up to 9 feet tall and 320 pounds. Native to Africa, it can't fly but runs up to 43 mph. Ostriches lay the largest eggs of any living land animal and have three stomachs to help digest tough plant matter.

Flamingo

Flamingos are famous for their pink plumage, which comes from carotenoid pigments in their diet of algae and crustaceans. They filter-feed with specialized beaks and often stand on one leg. There are six species found in Americas, Africa, Asia, and Europe.

Hawk

Hawks are medium-sized diurnal birds of prey with excellent vision (8 times sharper than humans). They have sharp talons for catching prey and hooked beaks for tearing flesh. There are over 200 species worldwide, including buzzards, eagles, and kites.

Albatross

Albatrosses are among the largest flying birds with wingspans up to 11 feet. They spend most of their life soaring over oceans and can sleep while flying. These monogamous birds mate for life and have the longest incubation period of any bird (about 80 days).

Pigeon

Pigeons are stout-bodied birds with short necks and slender bills. They're incredibly adaptable, found in cities worldwide. Pigeons can recognize themselves in mirrors, remember human faces, and navigate using the Earth's magnetic fields. They were the first domesticated bird.
