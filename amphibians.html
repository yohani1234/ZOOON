Amphibian Species

Indian Skittering Frog

The Indian Skittering Frog (Euphlyctis cyanophlyctis) is a common water frog found throughout South Asia. Known for its ability to skitter across water surfaces, it has a olive-green or brown coloration with dark spots and a distinctive white belly.

Indian Bullfrog

The Indian Bullfrog (Hoplobatrachus tigerinus) is one of the largest frogs in India, known for its deep resonant calls during monsoon. Males develop bright yellow coloration with blue vocal sacs during breeding season, while females remain olive green.

Indian Pond Frog

The Indian Pond Frog (Euphlyctis hexadactylus) is commonly found in freshwater habitats across India. It has a greenish-brown back with dark spots, a white belly, and distinctive fully webbed hind feet adapted for swimming.

Common Indian Toad

The Common Indian Toad (Duttaphrynus melanostictus) is widespread across India, recognizable by its dry, warty skin and parotoid glands behind the eyes. It's nocturnal and secretes a milky poison when threatened, serving as a defense mechanism.

Himalayan Toad

The Himalayan Toad (Duttaphrynus himalayanus) is found in the Himalayan foothills, adapted to cooler climates. It has a robust body, short limbs, and dark brown coloration with reddish warts. This species breeds in mountain streams during spring.

Purple Frog

The Purple Frog (Nasikabatrachus sahyadrensis) is a unique, bloated-looking frog endemic to the Western Ghats. With its purple-gray coloration and pointed snout, it spends most of its life underground, emerging only during monsoon for breeding.
